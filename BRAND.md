# rail-signal — Brand

Lightweight brand for a personal, product-register utility. (Not a marketing surface;
established inline rather than via a full discovery interview.)

## What it is
A pocket instrument that watches your cellular link on the commute, maps where it
dies, and fights to re-register when Android clings to a dead cell.

## Scene sentence
A regular commuter on a weak-signal rail line, phone in hand on a moving train, often in dim
morning/evening light, wants an instant read on whether the connection is alive and whether
the app is fighting for it — without squinting at numbers.

## Voice
Terse, instrument-like, honest. Status words over sentences (ALIVE / WEAK / DEAD / STUCK).
No marketing gloss, no exclamation. Numbers are the hero; prose is a label.

## Visual identity — "Signal Scope"
- **Register:** product (design serves the function).
- **Theme:** dark, tinted slate-ink (never pure black/gray). Glanceable in low light, OLED-kind.
- **Signature accent:** signal teal — reads "live". It doubles as the healthy end of the
  signal ramp, so a strong connection literally glows on-brand.
- **Signal-health ramp:** teal (alive) → amber (weak) → red (dead). Always semantic, regardless
  of chrome.
- **Hero:** a radial signal gauge + live RSRP sparkline. Instrument, not telecom-bars.
- **Numerals:** monospace (instrument readout). Chrome: system sans.
- **Deliberately off** the category reflex (telecom blue / Android green bars).
